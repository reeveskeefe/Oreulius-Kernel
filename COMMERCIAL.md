# Commercial Use

Oreulia is publicly available under the Oreulia Community License for
research, evaluation, modification, public discussion, and non-commercial
experimentation.

Commercial use requires a separate written agreement.

Commercial use typically includes:

- shipping Oreulia as part of a product sold to customers
- embedding Oreulia into proprietary systems or appliances
- operating Oreulia in production for a commercial entity
- offering hosted or managed services built on Oreulia
- providing paid support, integration, or consulting where Oreulia is a
  material part of the service

Internal company evaluation, testing, benchmarking, security review, and
prototype work are allowed under the Community License so long as Oreulia is
not used in production and is not part of a paid or revenue-generating
offering.

Commercial agreements can be structured around:

- product or platform deployment
- proprietary internal production use
- hosted service operation
- enterprise support or consulting
- custom modules or hardening work
